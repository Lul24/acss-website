The Crisis

The Hunger Crisis in South Sudan

South Sudan faces one of the world's most severe food crises. Over million people are food insecure, and acute malnutrition threatens the lives of children.

Conflict, displacement, climate shocks, and economic collapse have created a perfect storm of hunger. Children are the most vulnerable — malnutrition weakens immune systems, stunts physical growth, and impairs cognitive development permanently.

Our Approach

Integrated Nutrition Intervention

We tackle child malnutrition through a comprehensive approach — combining emergency response with long-term food security solutions.

MEAL

School Feeding

Daily nutritious meals at all our Accelerated Learning Centers — improving attendance, concentration, and learning outcomes for students.

SCRN

Malnutrition Screening & Treatment

Regular MUAC screening for all children in our care, with therapeutic feeding for severe acute malnutrition and supplementary feeding for moderate cases.

FARM

Agricultural Support & Livelihoods

Seeds, tools, and training for families to grow their own food — building resilience and reducing dependency on humanitarian aid.

EDUC

Nutrition Education

Community workshops on balanced nutrition, optimal infant and young child feeding practices, and household meal planning for caregivers.

How We Help

Our Nutrition Interventions

We address malnutrition at every level — from emergency treatment to sustainable food security.

Therapeutic Feeding

Emergency treatment for children with severe acute malnutrition using ready-to-use therapeutic foods (RUTF) and medical monitoring.

✅ 5% recovery rate 2-week treatment cycle

School Meals

Daily fortified meals at learning centers, ensuring children have the energy to learn and grow — with take-home rations for families.
